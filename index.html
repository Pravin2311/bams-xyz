FAQ - BAMS Study Guide & Ayurveda Learning

Common questions about ayurvedic medicine education, herbs database, dosha calculator and traditional healing methods

What is BAMS and how can this study guide help me?

BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery) is a comprehensive degree program in traditional Indian medicine. Our study guide provides 112 authentic medicinal herbs, 149 Sanskrit terminology, home remedies, and interactive tools to support your ayurvedic medicine education with authentic traditional knowledge and modern learning methods.

How accurate is the medicinal herbs database for BAMS students?

Our 112 herbs database contains authentic information sourced from classical ayurvedic texts including Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita, and contemporary research. Each herb entry includes Sanskrit names, properties, dosages, therapeutic applications, and dosha effects verified by qualified ayurvedic practitioners.

Can beginners use the dosha calculator and wellness tools?

Yes! Our free online dosha calculator and wellness tools are designed for both beginners and advanced practitioners. The questionnaire provides detailed explanations, and results include personalized diet recommendations, lifestyle guidance, and constitution analysis suitable for those new to ayurvedic principles.

What Sanskrit terms are included in the terminology dictionary?

Our comprehensive Sanskrit dictionary contains 149 essential ayurvedic terms covering anatomy, physiology, pathology, treatment principles, herb classifications, dosha theory, and diagnostic methods. Each term includes proper pronunciation, multiple meanings, and contextual usage in traditional medicine practice.

Are the home remedies safe for daily use?

Our 74 traditional home remedies are based on classical ayurvedic formulations using natural ingredients. However, always consult qualified healthcare practitioners before using any remedy, especially for chronic conditions or if you're taking medications. Individual constitution and health status should always be considered.

How do I learn pulse diagnosis (Nadi Pariksha) properly?

Our pulse analysis guide provides step-by-step instructions for traditional Nadi Pariksha techniques. Start with basic pulse reading fundamentals, practice regularly under guidance of experienced practitioners, and gradually develop sensitivity to detect dosha imbalances through pulse characteristics.

What makes this different from other ayurveda websites?

Our platform combines authentic traditional knowledge with modern digital tools specifically designed for BAMS students and practitioners. Features include downloadable herb cards, interactive calculators, comprehensive search functionality, mobile-optimized design, and verified content from classical texts.

How often is the content updated with new information?

We regularly update our database with new research findings, additional herbs, enhanced remedies, and improved tools. Content is reviewed quarterly by qualified ayurvedic practitioners to ensure accuracy and relevance for contemporary practice while maintaining traditional authenticity.

Is this suitable for ayurvedic practitioners and not just students?
